What is a Centralized Exchange (CEX)?

A Centralized Exchange (CEX) is a digital platform where users can buy, sell, and trade cryptocurrencies through a central authority or company. Popular examples include Binance, Coinbase, and Kraken.

In a CEX, the exchange acts as an intermediary. You deposit funds into your account on the platform, and they execute trades on your behalf. These platforms are usually regulated and offer fiat-to-crypto options.

Benefits:

  • ✅ Beginner-friendly interface
  • ✅ High liquidity and fast trades
  • ✅ Customer support and insurance (in some cases)

Risks:

  • ❌ You don’t control your private keys
  • ❌ Exchange can freeze or lose your funds
  • ❌ Subject to hacks or regulatory shutdowns

Real-World Analogy:

A centralized exchange is like a traditional bank. You deposit money, and they help you send, receive, or exchange it. But you rely on them to keep it safe and working.
