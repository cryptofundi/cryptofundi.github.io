Safety Tips for Trading and Storing Crypto

Crypto offers a powerful and open financial system—but with great freedom comes great responsibility. To stay safe, follow these best practices:

1. Use Reputable Wallets and Exchanges

  • Only trade on exchanges with a strong reputation (e.g., Coinbase, Binance, Kraken).
  • Use non-custodial wallets for long-term storage (e.g., MetaMask, Ledger, Trust Wallet).

2. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

  • Always enable 2FA for your exchange accounts and wallet apps.
  • Prefer app-based 2FA (like Google Authenticator) over SMS-based codes.

3. Use Cold Storage for Large Holdings

  • Cold wallets (offline devices like Ledger or Trezor) reduce the risk of online hacks.
  • Never store your seed phrase online or in the cloud.

4. Avoid Phishing and Scams

  • Always double-check URLs before entering sensitive info.
  • Never click on suspicious links from emails, social media, or DMs.
  • Verify official links using project websites or trusted sources like CoinGecko or CoinMarketCap.

5. Backup Your Wallet Securely

  • Write down your recovery phrase and store it in a secure, physical location.
  • Never share your seed phrase with anyone — no real service will ever ask for it.

6. Be Cautious with DApps

  • Use tools like revoke.cash to manage permissions you've granted to DApps.
  • Double-check the authenticity of DeFi protocols before interacting with them.

7. Keep Software Updated

  • Update your wallets, browser extensions, and operating systems regularly to patch vulnerabilities.

8. Consider Using a Hardware Wallet

For serious investors or those holding large amounts of crypto, a hardware wallet is the gold standard for secure storage.

Real-World Analogy:

Think of crypto safety like online banking. You wouldn't give out your bank PIN to a stranger, right? The same applies to your wallet seed phrase. Keep it secret, keep it safe.
