DEX vs CEX: Key Differences

In the world of crypto, exchanges come in two main forms: Centralized Exchanges (CEXs) and Decentralized Exchanges (DEXs). Understanding the difference helps you decide where and how to trade.

1. Control & Ownership

  • CEX: You deposit funds into the exchange. They control your crypto while it's stored or traded.
  • DEX: You always control your wallet and private keys. Trades happen directly from your wallet.

2. Ease of Use

  • CEX: Beginner-friendly interfaces and customer support.
  • DEX: May require some technical know-how (connecting wallet, setting gas fees, etc.).

3. Speed & Liquidity

  • CEX: Generally faster and more liquid. You get quick trades and tight spreads.
  • DEX: Can be slower and may have lower liquidity, especially for lesser-known tokens.

4. Privacy & Regulation

  • CEX: Often require KYC (identity verification).
  • DEX: No KYC. You remain anonymous and trade directly from your wallet.

Real-World Analogy:

A CEX is like a bank — it holds your money and handles transactions. A DEX is like using cash directly with a peer — no middleman, just you and the counterparty.
