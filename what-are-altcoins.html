What are Altcoins?

Altcoins are “alternative coins” — the name given to all cryptocurrencies other than Bitcoin.

💡 Why are they called Altcoins?

Bitcoin was the first cryptocurrency. Every coin that came after it was seen as an alternative — hence the name Alt-coin.

🪙 Popular Altcoins

  • Ethereum (ETH): Enables smart contracts and decentralized apps.
  • Solana (SOL): Known for its speed and low transaction fees.
  • Ripple (XRP): Designed for fast, global money transfers.
  • Polygon (MATIC): A layer-2 scaling solution for Ethereum.
  • Chainlink (LINK): Brings real-world data into blockchains.

🔍 What makes Altcoins different?

Each Altcoin usually tries to improve on some limitation of Bitcoin — whether it’s speed, energy efficiency, smart contracts, or use-case specialization.

💼 Categories of Altcoins

  • Stablecoins: Pegged to a real-world currency like USD (e.g., USDT, USDC)
  • Utility Tokens: Used for access or actions on a platform (e.g., BNB, UNI)
  • Governance Tokens: Let users vote on decisions (e.g., AAVE, COMP)
  • Meme Coins: Inspired by internet culture (e.g., Dogecoin, Pepe, Shiba Inu)

💬 Real-World Analogy

If Bitcoin is like digital gold, Altcoins are like different types of digital tools — each made for specific jobs or communities.

Bottom line: Altcoins offer diversity, innovation, and specialization in the crypto world. But they also carry different risks, so research before investing!
